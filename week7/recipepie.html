Martha's Apple Pie

A delicious family favorite passed down from generation to generation

Best Pie Apples: Transparents, Spies, Golden Delicious

Crust:

  • 2 1/4 Cup All-Purpose Flour
  • 1 Teaspoon Salt
  • 3/4 Cup and 2 Tablespoons Shortening
  • 5 Tablespoons Cold Water

Pie Filling:

  • 5-8 Large Apples
  • 3/4 - 1 Cup Granulated Sugar
  • 1 Tablespoon Flour
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Cinnamon
  • Butter Slices

    • In a bowl, mix flour and salt. With pastry blender, cut in shortening until size of peas. Sprinkle cold water on top of flour mixture. Keep mixing until a ball is made. Shape into flat round ball and cut in half. Form two even balls. Rool to fit into pie pan. Roll on floured counter flip over, measure to size of pan. Lift rolled crust into pan.

    Cut apples into dices. Mix in sugar, flour, and cinnamon. Arrange mixture in pie plate, should be heaping up. Dot top with margarine or butter slices and adjust top crust over pan. Decorate top crust with swirler. Crimp edges and trim off excess. With a sharp knife, cut four vents on top.

    Bake at 425 for 30-35 minutes. Or when bubbling over. Cover top with foil to prevent burning. If extra crust, roll into tarts and bake 10 min.

    Conversions

    3 teaspoons 1 tablespoon
    2 cups 1 pint
